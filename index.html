About me

The story so far

I still remember the first time I cleaned a massive dataset and made sense of it — that thrill never left.

I'm a Data Science grad student at RIT (GPA: 3.83), drawn to problems where the data is messy and the answers actually matter. From sentiment pipelines to healthcare dashboards, I've always been most alive when turning raw numbers into something people can act on.

As a Data Science Intern at Mercurial AI, I built an AI-powered voice assistant using WebRTC, developed semantic search pipelines over 1,000+ medical codes using Pinecone, and cut MRI image-processing time in half — from 180s to 90s.

Back at RIT, I serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for Neural Networks, fine-tuning LLMs and experimenting with few-shot prompting across DeepSeek and Gemini. I work across Python, SQL, NLP, and machine learning — but beyond the tools, I care about the why.
